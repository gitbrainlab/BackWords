# BackWords – Icon & Visual Identity Notes

---

## App Icon Concepts

### Concept A: Split Word
A word literally split in two halves — the left half in an aged parchment style (sepia, serif font), the right half in a clean modern sans-serif. A thin vertical line or gradient separates them. Works well with the app name "BackWords" as a concept of reversal and temporal split.

### Concept B: Speech Bubble + Clock
A speech bubble with a small clock or hourglass embedded in the lower-right corner. The speech bubble is slightly worn/textured on the left half, clean on the right. Conveys "language over time."

### Concept C: Bookmark + Timeline
An open book with a ribbon bookmark that extends into a horizontal timeline with dots. The current bookmark position is highlighted. Clean, literary, recognizable.

### Concept D: Waveform / Semantic Arc
An abstract arc rising from one point (past) to another (present) like a semantic journey. Could incorporate letterforms subtly. More conceptual, better for older audiences.

---

## Recommended Direction
**Concept A (Split Word)** with a secondary nod to Concept B. The split-word motif directly communicates the app's core proposition without requiring explanation.

### Execution Notes
- Use SF Pro Rounded for the modern half, a classical serif for the historical half
- Color: Scholarly palette gradient (terracotta left → current background right) on light; reverse for dark
- Rounded rectangle container at the standard iOS corner radius
- No text of the app name in the icon (per App Store guidelines)
